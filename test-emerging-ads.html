1. Best Smart Plug: TP-Link Kasa Mini ($14.99)

At just $15, the TP-Link Kasa Mini does everything you need from a smart plug and nothing you don't. Setup takes about 90 seconds through the Kasa app, and it works with all three major voice assistants without requiring a hub.

We particularly love the energy monitoring feature‚Äîit helped one tester identify a space heater that was costing them $47/month in standby power. The compact design means it won't block adjacent outlets, a common complaint with bulkier smart plugs.

Pros: Compact, reliable, excellent app, energy monitoring
Cons: No physical button for manual control
Best for: Lamps, fans, coffee makers, space heaters

2. Best Smart Bulb: Wyze Bulb Color ($7.99)

The Wyze Bulb Color is almost absurdly cheap for what it offers: 16 million colors, 1,100 lumens of brightness, and compatibility with all major smart home platforms. At $8 per bulb, you can outfit an entire room for less than the cost of a single Philips Hue bulb.

The catch? You need a 2.4GHz WiFi network (no 5GHz support), and the color accuracy isn't quite as precise as premium options. But for accent lighting, bedroom ambiance, or holiday decorations, it's unbeatable value.

3. Best Smart Display: Echo Show 5 (3rd Gen) ($89.99)

Amazon's Echo Show 5 is the Swiss Army knife of smart home control. The 5.5-inch touchscreen lets you view security cameras, control other devices, make video calls, and follow along with recipes‚Äîall for under $90.

The latest generation added a better speaker and a physical camera shutter for privacy. It's become the command center of our test smart home, sitting on the kitchen counter where it's always within reach.

4. Best Smart Lock: August Wi-Fi Smart Lock ($79.99)

The August Wi-Fi Smart Lock transforms your existing deadbolt into a smart lock in about 10 minutes. No need to change your keys or the external appearance of your door‚Äîthe August replaces only the interior thumbturn.

Auto-unlock (using your phone's location) works flawlessly about 95% of the time. The other 5%? You'll need to open the app manually. For most users, that's an acceptable trade-off for the convenience of never fumbling for keys again.

5. Best Robot Vacuum: Eufy RoboVac 11S ($99)

At the $99 price point, you're not getting LiDAR navigation or room mapping. What you are getting is a reliable vacuum that handles daily maintenance cleaning remarkably well. The 11S is whisper-quiet (55dB) and slim enough to fit under most furniture.

6. Best Video Doorbell: Blink Video Doorbell ($34.99)

The Blink Video Doorbell offers 1080p video, two-way audio, and works on batteries or existing doorbell wiring. At $35, it's less than a quarter of the price of a Ring Video Doorbell Pro‚Äîand for basic security monitoring, it does the job.

The main limitation is the lack of 24/7 recording (it only captures motion-triggered clips). But if you primarily want to see who's at your door before answering, it's perfect.

7. Best Smart Thermostat: Amazon Smart Thermostat ($79.99)

Co-developed with Honeywell, the Amazon Smart Thermostat brings Nest-like intelligence at a fraction of the price. It learns your schedule, adjusts for local weather, and integrates deeply with Alexa for voice control.

Most impressively, it supports utility energy-saving programs, potentially earning you rebates that bring the effective cost even lower. Our test home saw energy savings of about 12% over three months.

8. Best Smart Sensor: Aqara Door/Window Sensor ($15.99)

The Aqara Door/Window Sensor is tiny, reliable, and works with HomeKit, Google Home, and Alexa (via the Aqara hub, sold separately for $30). Use it to trigger automations when you leave home, get alerts if a door opens unexpectedly, or turn on lights when you enter a room.

At $16 each, you can afford to put them everywhere. Our test home has 14 of them monitoring every entry point, garage door, and medicine cabinet.
